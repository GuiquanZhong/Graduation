Intelligent Forum System Based on Spring Boot and Large Language Model Interface
ABSTRACT
With the explosive growth of Internet information, traditional forum systems are confronted with challenges such as information overload, uneven content quality, high thresholds for user creation, and low retrieval efficiency. To address these issues, this study designs and implements a modern intelligent forum system deeply integrated with large language model (LLM) services.
The system adopts a front-end and back-end separation architecture. The back-end is based on the mainstream Spring Boot framework, integrates the MyBatis-Plus module for efficient data persistence operations, and uses MySQL as the underlying storage database. The core innovation of this design lies in the construction of a "lightweight, pluggable" AI intelligent assistance module. By invoking APIs of third-party large language models such as MiniMax, the system realizes functions including automatic article summarization, AI-assisted title generation, intelligent text polishing, and open intelligent question answering.
Experiments show that the introduction of AI functions has significantly improved the accuracy of users' information acquisition, reduced the difficulty of content creation, and provided a referable engineering practice solution for the intelligent upgrading of traditional social platforms.
Key Words: Spring Boot; large language model; intelligent forum; API integration; MyBatis-Plus
